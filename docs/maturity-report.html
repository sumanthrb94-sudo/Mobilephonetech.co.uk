Front-end maturity report — benchmarking against ur.co.uk and Amazon UK.

Prepared: April 2026 · Source-of-truth: main branch · Assessment covers UX, catalogue, checkout, trust, accessibility, performance, and backend readiness.

Executive summary

Front-end capability: roughly 80% of ur.co.uk and 45% of Amazon UK in visible feature surface area.

Backend capability: approximately 5% of either peer — the site runs on mock data, no real payment processing, no real stock, no real accounts.

Net verdict: the site looks like a credible refurb specialist at ur.co.uk level. The moment a shopper attempts to transact, the mock backend becomes obvious.

Where you beat ur.co.uk today

  • AI Tech Advisor (Gemini-backed chat) — ur.co.uk has no AI assistant.
  • 6-frame Claude-designed gallery per product — ur.co.uk typically shows 3–4 photos only.
  • Price history chart with "Lowest in 90 days" trust badge — ur.co.uk does not surface price history.
  • Per-product environmental impact module (CO₂ / water / raw-material savings) — ur.co.uk has a generic sustainability page, not per-product data.
  • Finance tabs switching Klarna / Clearpay / Pay in 30 — ur.co.uk just mentions Klarna.
  • Content depth — About, Sustainability, Buying guides, FAQ centre all shipped.

Where Amazon beats everyone

The Amazon-class gaps are less "missing features" and more operational infrastructure you would need to build or buy. None of these are add-a-component scope — each is effectively a 6+ month infrastructure project.

  • Recommendation engine (ML serving layer)
  • Multi-seller marketplace
  • Real logistics with delivery-date guarantee
  • Prime-equivalent loyalty programme
  • Q&A per product with community moderation
  • Native mobile apps (iOS + Android)
  • Sponsored-ad inventory (revenue line)
  • A+ content creator tool for vendors
  • Bundles + subscribe & save
  • Enterprise customer support

Recommended next moves

To reach Phase 3 (ur.co.uk parity) on front-end

  1. Buy/Sell header toggle — small, high-visibility win. ~2 hrs.
  2. Live trade-in quote engine — multi-step wizard (brand → model → condition → storage → quote). Rules-based, no API needed for v1. ~1 day.
  3. Carrier filter on ProductsPage — add carrier field to Product, add filter chip. ~3 hrs.
  4. Price slider — replace min/max inputs with a headless range slider. ~2 hrs.

Total: ~2 days of focused work to reach ur.co.uk front-end parity.

To cross into real commerce (Phase 3 backend)

  1. Stripe integration — replaces the mock place-order with a real charge. 1–2 days.
  2. Supabase or Neon for DB (products, orders, users, reviews). 2–3 days.
  3. NextAuth or Clerk for real accounts. 1 day.
  4. Klarna / Clearpay merchant onboarding — paperwork, minimal code. Days elapsed, hours of dev.
  5. Real product feed ingestion (CSV → DB). Half a day.

~1.5 weeks to a real transactional site, assuming Stripe + merchant bank are already set up.

Bottom line

You have a Phase-3-ready front-end sitting on a Phase-0 backend. The UI is genuinely competitive with ur.co.uk today and punches above its weight with the AI advisor, eco module, price history, and 6-frame gallery.

The thing holding the site back from shipping to real customers is backend plumbing and real product / payment data — all of which are standard lifts, not design work.

End of report.
